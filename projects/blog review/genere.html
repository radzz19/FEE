Romantic fiction, also known as romance novels, is a genre of literature that emphasizes love, passion, and the relationship between characters.
It has been a popular form of literature for centuries, with authors such as Charles Dickens, Jane Austen, and Virginia Woolf contributing to the genre's richness and diversity.
Romantic fiction often features strong emotional tension, intense character development, and a focus on the complexities of human relationships.
